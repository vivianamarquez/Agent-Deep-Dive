California sea lions live along the western coast of North America, from British Columbia to Mexico.

They are social marine mammals and often rest together on docks, rocks, beaches, and buoys. They are known for loud barking calls, playful behavior, and excellent swimming ability.

Adult males are much larger than females and can develop a raised bump on the top of the head called a sagittal crest. California sea lions use their strong front flippers to swim and can also rotate their rear flippers forward to move on land.

Their diet includes fish, squid, and other small marine animals. They are opportunistic hunters and may follow schools of fish.
