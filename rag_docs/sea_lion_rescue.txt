Sea lion rescue centers care for animals that are injured, sick, malnourished, entangled in fishing gear, or separated from their mothers.

Rescue teams usually ask the public to keep a safe distance from stranded sea lions. A sea lion on the beach is not always in trouble, because healthy animals may come ashore to rest.

People should not feed, touch, pour water on, or push a sea lion back into the ocean. These actions can stress the animal and may be dangerous for both the sea lion and the person.

If a sea lion appears injured or distressed, the best response is to contact a local marine mammal rescue organization and describe the animal's location and condition.
