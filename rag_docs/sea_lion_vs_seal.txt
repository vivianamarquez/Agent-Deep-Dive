Sea lions and seals are related, but they are not the same animal.

Sea lions have visible external ear flaps. Most true seals do not have visible ear flaps.

Sea lions can rotate their rear flippers under their bodies, which helps them walk or climb on land. True seals usually move on land by wiggling or scooting because their rear flippers point backward.

Sea lions are often noisier than seals and may gather in large, loud groups. Their front flippers are long and powerful, making them agile swimmers.
